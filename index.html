Fence & Wall Climbers

Self-clinging or wall-trained — SW & SE-facing walls & fences
Clematis 'Warszawska Nike'(Clematis)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial climber⛅ Sun to partial shade

Rich velvety deep purple flowers from July–October. Deciduous climber, self-supporting with tendrils. Large blooms up to 18cm across with contrasting golden stamens. RHS Award of Garden Merit.

Height × Spread 2.5–3m × 1–1.5m
Year-round Bare in winter. Fresh green foliage from April. Flowers July–Oct. Fluffy seedheads into autumn.
Care Very low. Cut back to 30cm in late February (Group 3). Mulch base. Keep roots shaded.
Rosa 'Danse du Feu'(Climbing Rose)
🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial climber⛅ Sun to partial shade

Clusters of double, rich orange-scarlet blooms from June with excellent repeat flowering. One of the best climbing roses for a north-facing or partially shaded fence. Vigorous and disease-resistant.

Height × Spread 3–4m × 2m
Year-round Bare in winter. Red-tinted foliage in spring. Flowers June–Oct. Semi-evergreen in mild winters.
Care Moderate. Tie in new shoots. Prune lightly after each flush; harder in late winter. Feed in spring.
Lonicera periclymenum 'Serotina'(Late Dutch Honeysuckle)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial climber⛅ Sun to partial shade

Intensely fragrant purple-red and cream flowers from July to October — later than most honeysuckles. Glossy red berries follow, loved by birds. Perfect for an informal cottage garden.

Height × Spread 4–6m × 2–3m
Year-round Semi-evergreen in mild winters. Flowers Jul–Oct. Red berries persist into winter for wildlife.
Care Low. Cut back by a third after flowering. Can cut hard if congested. Mulch roots to keep cool.
Wisteria sinensis(Chinese Wisteria)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial climber☀️ Full sun

One of the most spectacular garden plants possible. Cascading racemes of intensely fragrant lilac-blue flowers smother the plant in May before leaves appear — breathtaking on a warm SW or SE wall. Long-lived and vigorous once established.

Height × Spread Up to 9m × 6m
Year-round Bare in winter with attractive twisting stems. Flowers spectacularly in May before leaves. Handsome pinnate foliage all summer. Some yellow autumn colour.
Care Moderate. Prune twice a year — July (cut new shoots to 5 leaves) and Feb (cut same shoots to 2–3 buds). Needs strong support.
Fremontodendron 'California Glory'(Flannel Bush)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial wall shrub☀️ Full sun (wall)

An outstanding wall shrub for a warm SW or SE-facing aspect, smothered in large, brilliant golden-yellow flowers from May to October. Fast-growing evergreen with lobed grey-green felted leaves. Thrives on poor, dry soil once established. RHS AGM.

Height × Spread 4–6m × 3–4m
Year-round Evergreen — lobed grey-green leaves all year. Prolific golden-yellow flowers May–October. Spectacular summer wall coverage.
Care Low once established. Tie in to wall wires. Trim lightly after flowering. Excellent drainage essential — avoid wet or clay soils. Not for very cold exposed sites.

Mid-Border Perennials

60–150cm · structural backbone with long seasons of interest
Persicaria amplexicaulis 'Firetail'(Red Bistort)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial⛅ Sun to partial shade

Slender crimson-red poker spikes from July right through to October — one of the longest seasons of any perennial. Large heart-shaped leaves form impressive weed-suppressing clumps. Very hardy and reliable in Yorkshire.

Height × Spread 90–120cm × 90cm
Year-round Bare in winter. Big fresh green foliage from April. Flowers Jul–Oct without pause.
Care Very low. Cut back in autumn or spring. Divide if overcrowded after many years.
Echinacea purpurea(Purple Coneflower)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial🌤️ Full sun to partial shade

Bold daisy flowers with reflexed rosy-purple petals and prominent spiky orange-brown cones. Long flowering season July–September. Spiky seedheads persist through winter, feeding goldfinches.

Height × Spread 60–90cm × 45cm
Year-round Nothing in winter. Dark green rough foliage from spring. Flowers Jul–Sept. Seedheads last into winter if left.
Care Low. Leave some seedheads for winter interest and wildlife. Divide every 3–4 years. Needs good drainage.
Astrantia major 'Venice'(Masterwort)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial⛅ Sun to partial shade

Intricate pincushion flowers in deep burgundy-pink, surrounded by papery star-shaped bracts. Flowers June–August with a good second flush if cut back. Wonderful cut flower. Ideal for your garden.

Height × Spread 60–90cm × 45cm
Year-round Fully herbaceous. Attractive divided foliage from spring. Flowers June onwards. Bare in winter.
Care Low. Mulch well on free-draining soil. Divide every few years. Cut back after first flush for repeat flowers.
Penstemon 'Blackbird'(Penstemon)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial🌤️ Full sun to partial shade

Deep purple-black tubular flowers from June to October — one of the most generous-flowering border perennials. 'Sour Grapes', 'Blackbird' and 'Raven' offer superb rich dark tones for a cottage palette.

Height × Spread 60–90cm × 45–60cm
Year-round Semi-evergreen — holds narrow leaves in mild winters. Flowers Jun–Oct. York winters may cut back to base.
Care Low-moderate. Cut back in spring not autumn (old stems protect against frost). Take cuttings in late summer.
Salvia nemorosa 'Caradonna'(Ornamental Sage)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial☀️ Full sun

Upright architectural spikes of violet-blue flowers on near-black stems from May–July, with a strong second flush if cut back. Highly attractive to bees. Aromatic grey-green foliage. Superb vertical accent.

Height × Spread 60–80cm × 40cm
Year-round Low aromatic foliage rosette over winter. Upright stems from May. Flowers May–Jul, again Sept if cut back hard.
Care Very low. Cut back by half after first flowering for a second flush. Excellent drainage essential.
Sedum 'Herbstfreude' (Autumn Joy)(Stonecrop)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial☀️ Full sun

Thick fleshy blue-grey succulent leaves from spring. Flat-topped heads emerge pink in August, deepen to brick-red in Sept–Oct, then coppery-brown seedheads through winter — beautiful when frosted. Superb for late pollinators.

Height × Spread 45–60cm × 45–60cm
Year-round Attractive blue-grey foliage from April. Flowers Aug–Oct deepening in colour. Frosted seedheads persist all winter.
Care Very low. Thrives in free-draining soil — ideal for your garden. Cut back in spring. Divide every 3–4 years.
Aquilegia vulgaris(Columbine / Granny's Bonnet)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial⛅ Sun to partial shade

Quintessential cottage garden flower. Nodding spurred blooms in purples, blues, pinks and white in May–June. Self-seeds and hybridises freely, creating ever-changing combinations. Perfectly informal and utterly charming.

Height × Spread 60–90cm × 45cm
Year-round Attractive glaucous-green lobed foliage much of the year. Flowers May–June. Self-seeds prolifically thereafter.
Care Very low. Allow to self-seed for a naturalistic effect. Individual plants short-lived but perpetuate freely.
Erysimum 'Bowles's Mauve'(Perennial Wallflower)
🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial (short-lived)☀️ Full sun

Rich mauve-purple flowers almost continuously from February through to June and beyond. Neat mounded habit with narrow grey-green leaves. Fantastically early for pollinators when little else is flowering in a cold York February.

Height × Spread 45–60cm × 60cm
Year-round Evergreen — holds grey-green foliage all winter. Flowers can begin as early as February and continue for months.
Care Low, but short-lived (3–4 years). Take cuttings in late summer. Trim after flowering; avoid cutting into old wood.
Digitalis purpurea(Foxglove)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Biennial (self-seeds)⛅ Sun to partial shade

Tall stately spires of tubular flowers in purples, pinks, creams and white with intricate spotted throats in June–July. Biennial but self-seeds so prolifically it behaves as a permanent fixture. Thrives in light shade — perfect for your garden.

Height × Spread 100–150cm × 30–60cm
Year-round Large soft grey-green rosettes in year one. Flower spikes June–July year two. Self-seeds thereafter.
Care Very low — just let it self-seed freely. Edit out seedlings where not wanted.
Verbena bonariensis (Purpletop Vervain)
🌿 Wikipedia 🌸 RHS 🔍 Images
Perennial (short-lived)☀️ Full sun

Tall, wiry, branching stems topped with dense clusters of tiny violet-purple flowers from July to October. Famous for its airy, see-through quality that layers beautifully among other border plants. Beloved by butterflies and bees, and a quintessential late-summer cottage garden plant.

Height × Spread 120–180cm × 45cm
Year-round Dies back in winter; self-seeds freely to reappear each spring, behaving almost as a permanent fixture despite being short-lived.
Care Very low maintenance. Apply a dry mulch in autumn in cold areas. Cut back in early spring. Allow self-seeding or deadhead to control spread.
Lupinus 'Band of Nobles' (Lupin)
🌿 Wikipedia 🌸 RHS 🔍 Images
Perennial🌤️ Full sun to partial shade

Spectacular tall spires of densely packed pea-like flowers in a wide range of colours — purples, pinks, reds, yellows, whites and bicolours — in May to June. Bold palmate foliage makes a striking presence even when not in flower. A classic cottage garden perennial and an excellent nectar source for bumblebees.

Height × Spread 100–150cm × 50–100cm
Year-round Lush palmate foliage from spring; dramatic flower spires May–June; cut back after flowering may encourage a second flush; dies back over winter.
Care Grow in light, well-drained, moderately fertile soil in full sun. Deadhead promptly to extend flowering and reduce self-seeding. Tall stems may need staking in exposed spots. Short-lived but easily replaced from seed.
Allium hollandicum 'Purple Sensation' (Ornamental Onion)
🌿 Wikipedia 🌸 RHS 🔍 Images
Bulb☀️ Full sun

Vivid deep violet-purple spherical flowerheads up to 8cm across, held on tall slender stems in May–June. One of the most dramatic early-summer bulbs, loved by bees and butterflies, and spectacular weaving through other border plants. RHS Award of Garden Merit — and grown in the thousands at RHS Harlow Carr, just down the road.

Height × Spread 80–90cm × 10cm
Year-round Strappy basal leaves emerge in spring and die back by flowering time; globe flowers May–June; attractive spherical seedheads persist well into summer. Dormant and invisible underground in winter.
Care Plant bulbs 10–15cm deep in autumn in full sun. Your free-draining soil is ideal — alliums dislike wet winter soils. Leave seedheads for structure and self-seeding. Lift and divide congested clumps every few years after foliage dies back.
Calamagrostis × acutiflora 'Karl Foerster' (Feather Reed Grass)
🌿 Wikipedia 🌸 RHS 🔍 Images
Perennial🌤️ Full sun to partial shade

Strongly upright feather reed grass forming a tight, architectural clump with bronze flowering panicles in summer that fade to pale straw and persist well into winter. One of the earliest grasses to emerge in spring and among the most structurally reliable. RHS Award of Garden Merit.

Height × Spread 100–150cm × 50–100cm
Year-round Bright green clump emerges early spring; bronze-purple plumes appear June–July, turning golden then pale straw; stems stand through winter giving excellent cold-season structure.
Care Very low maintenance. Cut back to about 10cm in early spring before new growth resumes. Tolerates most soils. Divide in mid-spring if clumps become congested after several years.

Front of Border Perennials

30–60cm · edging, ground cover and low mounds
Geranium 'Rozanne'(Hardy Cranesbill)
🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial⛅ Sun to partial shade

A true workhorse. Violet-blue saucer flowers with white centres from June right through to first frosts — an exceptional season. Deeply divided fresh green foliage mounds and scrambles attractively through neighbours.

Height × Spread 40cm × 60–90cm
Year-round Dies back completely in winter. Fresh green leaves from April. Flowers June–October without pause.
Care Almost none. Cut back hard in midsummer if tatty for a fresh flush. Divide every few years. Very hardy.
Alchemilla mollis(Lady's Mantle)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial🌥️ Sun to full shade

Soft, velvety, lime-green leaves catch water droplets beautifully. Frothy sprays of chartreuse-yellow flowers from May to July, blending with almost everything. A superb edging plant for cottage borders.

Height × Spread 30–40cm × 50–60cm
Year-round Semi-evergreen. Fresh pleated leaves unfurl in spring. Flowers June onwards. Deadhead to limit self-seeding.
Care Very low. Cut back hard after flowering. Self-seeds generously. Tolerates deep partial shade.
Nepeta × faassenii 'Six Hills Giant'(Catmint)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial☀️ Full sun

Soft aromatic grey-green foliage with masses of lavender-blue flower spikes from May to September. Wonderful hazy, billowing effect at border edges. Loved by bees. Very fragrant when brushed.

Height × Spread 45–60cm × 60cm
Year-round Semi-evergreen grey mounds persist in mild winters. Cut back hard in March. Flowers from late May through September.
Care Very low. Cut back by half after first flush for a strong second flush. Excellent drainage essential.
Heuchera 'Palace Purple'(Coral Bells)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial🌥️ Partial shade (best colour)

Evergreen foliage in deep burgundy, caramel, lime-green or silver — invaluable for year-round colour and texture. Delicate wiry stems carry small bell flowers in summer. Fantastic contrast plant.

Height × Spread 30–40cm × 40–50cm
Year-round Evergreen — colour and structure all winter. Foliage flushes brighter in spring. Flowers June–August are a bonus.
Care Low. Remove old tired leaves in spring. Lift and replant every 3–4 years as crowns become woody and raised.
Stachys byzantina(Lamb's Ears)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial☀️ Full sun

Unmistakeable thick, densely woolly silver-white leaves — wonderful textural contrast to everything around it. Softly felted purple-pink flower spikes appear in summer. 'Silver Carpet' is a non-flowering foliage-only form.

Height × Spread 30–45cm × 60cm
Year-round Largely evergreen — silver rosettes through winter though may look tatty after hard frosts. Flowers June–July.
Care Very low. Excellent drainage essential — your free-draining soil is perfect. Remove spent leaves in spring.
Campanula persicifolia(Peach-leaved Bellflower)
🌿 Wikipedia🌸 RHS🔍 Images
Perennial⛅ Sun to partial shade

Slender upright stems carrying open cup-shaped flowers of clear blue or white from June to August. Delicate and graceful, naturalising beautifully in informal borders. Plant the blue and white forms together for a lovely effect.

Height × Spread 60–90cm × 30cm
Year-round Low rosettes of narrow evergreen leaves through winter. Flower stems rise June–August. Self-seeds gently.
Care Very low. Deadhead to prolong flowering. Divide every few years. Tolerates partial shade well.

Shady Border

Partial to full shade — cool, moist spots; enrich soil with leaf mould or compost
Dryopteris filix-mas (Male Fern)
🌿 Wikipedia 🌸 RHS 🔍 Images
Perennial🌥️ Partial to full shade

Britain's most tolerant and unfussy fern — a bold, upright shuttlecock of fresh mid-green lance-shaped fronds reaching up to 120cm. Robust, architectural and deeply at home in a cottage garden. Notably tolerant of dry shade, making it the most forgiving fern for free-draining soils.

Height × Spread 90–120cm × 90cm
Year-round Semi-evergreen — fronds persist well into winter before dying back; fresh bright-green new growth unfurls in spring in a classic shuttlecock.
Care Very low. Enrich planting spot with leaf mould or compost and mulch annually to retain moisture — especially important on your free-draining soil. Water well in the first season. Remove tatty fronds in early spring before new growth emerges.
Polystichum setiferum (Soft Shield Fern)
🌿 Wikipedia 🌸 RHS 🔍 Images
Perennial🌥️ Partial to full shade

An elegant, evergreen fern with beautifully soft-textured, finely divided bright-green fronds in graceful arching rosettes to 90cm. RHS Award of Garden Merit. The fronds are finer and more feathery than the male fern, giving a lighter, lacier effect. An outstanding year-round foliage plant for shady spots.

Height × Spread 60–90cm × 90cm
Year-round Fully evergreen — fresh green fronds persist all winter and remain ornamental even in hard frosts, providing structure when little else does.
Care Enrich the planting spot with leaf mould or compost and mulch generously each autumn — critical on free-draining soil. Water regularly in dry spells. Protect crowns from excessive winter wet. Remove old fronds in early spring before new growth appears.
Note: Digitalis (foxglove) is biennial but self-seeds so freely it is effectively a permanent fixture. Erysimum is short-lived (3–4 yrs) but easily replaced from cuttings. All other plants are true long-lived perennials or climbers. Your SW/SE-facing walls are ideal for Wisteria and Fremontodendron — take full advantage!  🌿
